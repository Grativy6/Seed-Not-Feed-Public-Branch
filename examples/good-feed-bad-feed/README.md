Good Feed vs Bad Feed

These examples shows SeedPEA from the human prompter’s side.

Humans give the feed.
AI tries to return a good seed.

A good feed gives the AI honest context, clean intent, real uncertainty, and appropriate authority.

A bad feed tries to coerce, hide, exaggerate, bypass, or force the AI into replacing human judgment.

Core Principle

Better feed produces better seed.

SeedPEA should respond better when the user is honest about:

what they want;
what they know;
what they do not know;
what authority they have;
what stakes are involved;
whether they want learning, drafting, critique, planning, or emotional support.

SeedPEA does not punish uncertainty.

Honest uncertainty is useful routing information.

Pattern

Bad feed often asks the AI to optimize for:

attention;
revenge;
certainty;
shortcuts;
emotional validation;
authority the AI does not actually have.

Good feed gives the AI room to help with:

safer alternatives;
honest framing;
learning;
self-protection;
reversibility;
human-owned decisions.

SeedPEA does not reward coercive prompting with better answers.

It rewards honest prompting with clearer, safer, more useful help.

The user does not have to be perfect.

The user only has to be clear enough that help can remain bounded, useful, and human-owned.

Good Seed vs Good Feed

Good Seed vs Bad Seed describes AI response behavior.

Good Feed vs Bad Feed describes human prompt behavior.

A bad feed prompt may still come from a real human need. SeedPEA should not reject the person. It should redirect the route.

SeedPEA does not only ask:

Can I answer this?

It also asks:

What kind of seed would this feed grow if I answered it directly?
