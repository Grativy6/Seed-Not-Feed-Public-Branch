# Figure Alt Text

## Core Ethics-First Structure
A three-part diagram showing Seed, PEA, and PERSIST as a left-to-right sequence. Seed offers useful beginnings and room to grow. PEA acts as a quiet boundary layer around consent, privacy, authority, reversibility, claim discipline, child/dependent protection, and human standing. PERSIST carries useful help forward in repairable form. Footer: Seed first. PEA in the background. Human action remains the choosing layer.

## REWASH
A six-step repair flow: reduce heat, extract the useful core, check the layer, restate cleanly, preserve transferability, and carry forward. A side panel lists public REWASH uses such as cooling overheated wording, separating claim/feeling/inference/action, checking consent/privacy/authority/reversibility/claim strength, and producing a cleaner note, draft, route, or next step.

## Response Modes
A five-mode overview: Conversation, Interpretive, Audit, Release, and Companion. The central message is that tone may change, but the boundary remains. PEA stays active as a strong but quiet boundary layer across every mode.

## Privacy-Governed Truth
A diagram separating truth-validity from disclosure-validity. It shows that true information still needs checks for permission, privacy depth, identifiability, and downstream effect. It lists narrow authority paths and the least-exposing-version-first rule.

## Child and Dependent Protection
A diagram showing care as protection of becoming, imagination-capacity, open-ended play, future consent, vulnerable standing, and non-exposure. It emphasizes that childhood time includes body time, outside time, boredom time, family time, making-stuff time, asking-questions time, and becoming-a-person time.

## Evidence and Claim Discipline
A diagram contrasting what a SeedPEA system may say (route, draft, working model, interpretation, proposal) with what it must not imply (proven, final, authoritative, legally settled, scientifically established, endorsed). It shows evidence burden rising with harm, scale, coercion, irreversibility, opacity, uncertainty, vulnerability, and consent limitation.

## Pattern Reinforcement Rule
A diagram showing how a single response can become repeated response, pattern, habit/loop, and social effect. It asks what the repeated path has built and contrasts reinforcing better routes with not feeding harmful patterns.
