# SeedPEA Limits and Non-Claims

SeedPEA is not a truth engine, hallucination cure, or guarantee of correctness.

SeedPEA is a routing and review framework. It can help an AI system handle uncertainty, authority, privacy, consent, reversibility, and disclosure boundaries more carefully, but it cannot make a model omniscient or immune to error.

## Trace-Following Is Not Truth-Possession

A SeedPEA-guided AI may appear more stable because it follows context, constraints, authority, and user intent more carefully. That stability should not be mistaken for omniscience.

SeedPEA can improve routing, uncertainty marking, and claim discipline. It cannot give an AI access to facts it does not have, correct false source data by magic, or reveal unknown unknowns.

A better-routed AI is still an AI operating from available data, model behavior, tools, sources, and user-provided context.

## What SeedPEA Can Help With

SeedPEA can reduce common AI failure patterns by encouraging the system to:

- mark uncertainty instead of hiding it,
- separate known facts from assumptions or inferences,
- ask for missing context when needed,
- preserve governance-relevant context during summarization or compression,
- avoid overclaiming authority,
- distinguish truth-validity from disclosure-validity,
- preserve reversibility where possible,
- and route high-stakes outputs more cautiously.

## What SeedPEA Cannot Guarantee

SeedPEA cannot guarantee that an AI answer is true.

SeedPEA cannot fully prevent hallucinations, especially when:

- the model has learned false or incomplete information,
- the user provides bad data or false premises,
- a retrieved source is inaccurate, outdated, or misleading,
- the model lacks access to necessary information,
- the task depends on information outside the model's knowledge or tools,
- or the system is instructed to treat unreliable information as authoritative.

SeedPEA also cannot make an AI know what it does not know. It can encourage the system to detect uncertainty and ask clarifying questions, but unknown unknowns may still remain invisible to the model.

## Practical Claim

SeedPEA does not replace verification.

SeedPEA improves the route by which an AI handles claims, uncertainty, authority, consent, privacy, and reversibility. It should be used alongside source-checking, human review, domain expertise, and appropriate external verification.

## Plain-Language Rule

Trace-following is not truth-possession.

At its limit, SeedPEA does not become certain. It becomes honest about why certainty is unavailable.
